Introduction

This document presents the genealogical record of the Makower family, tracing the lineage from Dawid Makower (born circa 1760, Plock, Poland) through eight generations to the present day. The family's story spans the full arc of modern Jewish history — from 19th-century Poland, through the devastation of the Holocaust, to the establishment of the State of Israel, and today to communities in Tel Aviv, Toronto, and New Jersey.

This tree was compiled from cemetery records, JRI-Poland vital records, Yad Vashem Pages of Testimony, and living family members. Approximate dates are indicated with a tilde (~).

A note on completeness: Some individuals have limited information, particularly married-in spouses in earlier generations and individuals lost in the Holocaust. Gaps represent missing records, not missing people. Contributions from family members are always welcome.

Family Statistics

Tree Structure

The tree is organized into eight generations (Gen 1–8):

  • Generation 1 — Dawid Makower & Rachel (c. 1760–1771, Plock). Earliest known ancestors.
  • Generation 2 — Tanchum Makower & Zelda (c. 1823–1830, Plock).
  • Generation 3 — David Makower & Hadasa Schwarzberg (c. 1865–1870, Plock). The pivotal generation from whom all modern branches descend.
  • Generation 4 — David and Hadasa's eight children, each founding a distinct branch: Tanchum, Isaac Yacob, Baruch, Rachel, Yosef, Frieda, Zipora, and Ester.
  • Generations 5–8 — Descendants primarily in Israel, with branches in South Africa, Toronto (Canada), and New Jersey (USA).

Naming Conventions & Date Formatting

Names

Full names are used throughout. Where a person is known by both a Hebrew and a secular name (e.g., Hadassah/Ada Makower), both are recorded as Full Name (Common Name).

Dates

Birth and death years are four-digit years (e.g., 1933). Approximate years carry a tilde prefix: ~1925. Deaths in the Holocaust are recorded as "Holocaust" rather than a specific year, reflecting uncertainty and the nature of those deaths.

Locations

Locations follow the format City, Country using modern country names (e.g., Plock, Poland; Kfar-Hess, Israel; Toronto, Canada; New Jersey, USA).

Holocaust Notes

This section addresses family members who perished in the Holocaust, presented with care and respect for their memory.

The Holocaust devastated the Makower family. Of David and Hadasa's eight children, at least four branches were entirely destroyed. The following ten family members are confirmed victims:

  • Isaac Yacob Makower (b. 1890) — Lubic, Poland
  • Hinda Koral (b. 1895, wife of Isaac Yacob)
  • Hadassah (Ada) Makower (b. ~1925, daughter of Isaac Yacob) — Babi-Yar, Ukraine, 1941
  • Baruch Makower (b. 1894) — Warsaw, 1942
  • Lea Lichtenstein (b. 1905, wife of Baruch)
  • Hadassah Makower (b. 1926, daughter of Baruch) — Warsaw, 1942, age 16
  • Bracha Makower (b. 1931, daughter of Baruch) — Warsaw, 1942, age 11
  • Rachel Makower (b. 1904) — Warsaw, 1942
  • Yitschak Berenstein (b. 1905, husband of Rachel)
  • Hadassah Berenstein (b. ~1932, daughter of Rachel) — Warsaw, 1942, age ~10

Alexander Makower (b. 1923, son of Isaac Yacob) survived and emigrated to Israel, passing away in Beer-Sheva in 2005.

Branch Guide

  • ■ Tanchum branch — Kfar-Hess, Israel. Yigal Makower and family.
  • ■ Isaac Yacob branch — Survived through Alexander Makower. Large branch: Beer-Sheva, Caesarea, Macabim, Tel Aviv, Toronto, New Jersey.
  • ■ Baruch branch — Entirely lost in the Holocaust. No surviving descendants.

  • ■ Zipora branch — Kfar-Hess, Israel. Yakoby family descendants.

  • ■ Yosef branch — No known descendants recorded.
